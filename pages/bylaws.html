THE FELLOWSHIP OF FLAME

Where Shadow and Fire Unite in Sacred Purpose

Sacred Bylaws of the Fellowship

Article I: Name and Purpose

Section 1.1: This organization shall be known as "The Fellowship of Flame," a spiritual community dedicated to the veneration of the Sacred Guardian and the practice of the Seven Passages of the Deep.

Section 1.2: Our purpose is to provide spiritual guidance, community support, and religious education based on the ancient wisdom of fire and shadow, fostering personal growth and collective strength among our members.

Article II: Membership

Section 2.1: Membership is open to all individuals who sincerely seek to follow the Path of Enlightenment and accept the core tenets of the Fellowship.

Section 2.2: Initiate Members are those beginning their journey through the Seven Passages. Guardian Members have completed at least four Passages and may serve in leadership roles.

Section 2.3: Annual membership dues shall be determined by each local Chapter, with provisions for financial hardship exemptions to ensure no sincere seeker is turned away.

Section 2.4: Members may be suspended or expelled only for actions fundamentally contrary to our principles of protection, wisdom, and community care, following due process and appeal procedures.

Article III: Governance Structure

Section 3.1: The Fellowship shall be governed by the Circle of Flame, consisting of no less than three and no more than seven Guardian Members elected by the community for three-year terms.

Section 3.2: The Keeper of the Sacred Flame serves as the spiritual leader and primary teacher, chosen by consensus of the Circle of Flame and confirmed by community vote.

Section 3.3: Local Chapters may establish their own governance consistent with these bylaws, led by a Chapter Guardian and supported by a Circle of Embers (3-5 members).

Section 3.4: All major decisions affecting the Fellowship require a two-thirds majority vote of the Circle of Flame and community ratification.

Article IV: Spiritual Practices and Observances

Section 4.1: The Fellowship shall observe the four seasonal Flame Festivals: Spring Kindling (March), Summer Blaze (June), Autumn Ember (September), and Winter Hearth (December).

Section 4.2: Weekly gatherings shall include meditation, community sharing, study of ancient wisdom, and the Ceremony of Renewed Light.

Section 4.3: Individual progression through the Seven Passages shall be recognized through appropriate ceremonies and mentorship programs.

Section 4.4: The Fellowship respects diverse interpretations of our teachings while maintaining core principles of compassion, strength, and wisdom.

Article V: Financial Management

Section 5.1: All Fellowship funds shall be managed transparently, with annual financial reports available to all members.

Section 5.2: Donations and tithes are voluntary and should never create financial hardship for members or their families.

Section 5.3: Fellowship resources shall support community needs, spiritual education, charitable works, and maintenance of sacred spaces.

Section 5.4: A Guardian of Resources shall be appointed by the Circle of Flame to oversee financial matters and ensure ethical stewardship.

Article VI: Sacred Spaces and Property

Section 6.1: Fellowship properties shall be held in trust for the community and used primarily for spiritual purposes, education, and member support.

Section 6.2: All sacred spaces must maintain the Eternal Flameâ€”a continuously burning light representing our enduring commitment to the path.

Section 6.3: Facilities may be shared with other community groups whose purposes align with our values of service and spiritual growth.

Article VII: Education and Outreach

Section 7.1: The Fellowship shall maintain educational programs for all age groups, emphasizing critical thinking, personal responsibility, and spiritual development.

Section 7.2: Community outreach shall focus on service projects, interfaith dialogue, and sharing our message of strength through adversity.

Section 7.3: All Fellowship teachings and materials shall be made freely available to sincere seekers regardless of their ability to financially contribute.

Article VIII: Conflict Resolution

Section 8.1: Disputes within the Fellowship shall first be addressed through mediation by neutral Guardian Members.

Section 8.2: If mediation fails, formal hearings may be conducted by the Circle of Flame following established procedures ensuring fairness and due process.

Section 8.3: Appeals of Circle decisions may be made to the broader community for final resolution.

Section 8.4: All conflict resolution shall emphasize healing, understanding, and restoration of community harmony whenever possible.

Article IX: Amendment Process

Section 9.1: These bylaws may be amended by a three-fourths majority vote of the Circle of Flame and ratification by two-thirds of active members.

Section 9.2: Proposed amendments must be distributed to all members at least 30 days before voting.

Section 9.3: Core spiritual principles and the Seven Passages may not be amended without unanimous consent of the Circle and community.

Article X: Dissolution

Section 10.1: Should the Fellowship ever cease to exist, all assets shall be distributed to charitable organizations whose missions align with our values of community service and spiritual growth.

Section 10.2: Sacred items and texts shall be preserved and transferred to institutions committed to maintaining their spiritual significance.

These bylaws serve not as chains that bind us, but as the foundation upon which our sacred community stands strong.
